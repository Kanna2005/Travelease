Festivals of India

Experience the vibrant colors, rich traditions, and joyous celebrations that make India's festivals truly unforgettable. Find the perfect festival to plan your travel around.

Oct-Nov

Diwali

Nationwide

The festival of lights, celebrating the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. Homes are decorated with lamps, fireworks light up the sky, and families exchange sweets.

Hindu Lights Fireworks

Travel Tips

  • Best places: Varanasi, Jaipur, Amritsar
  • Book accommodations 3-4 months in advance
  • Try traditional sweets like laddoos and barfis
  • Be mindful of noise from fireworks
Mar

Holi

Nationwide

The festival of colors marks the arrival of spring. People throw colored powders and water at each other, dance to music, and celebrate with special foods and drinks.

Hindu Colors Spring

Travel Tips

  • Top spots: Mathura, Vrindavan, Barsana
  • Wear clothes you don't mind getting stained
  • Use organic colors to protect your skin
  • Attend the Lathmar Holi in Barsana for unique celebrations
Sep-Oct

Durga Puja

West Bengal

A grand celebration honoring Goddess Durga's victory over the buffalo demon Mahishasura. Elaborate pandals (temporary structures) house beautiful idols worshipped for five days.

Hindu Art Cultural

Travel Tips

  • Kolkata is the epicenter of celebrations
  • Visit different pandals to see unique themes
  • Try Bengali street food like phuchka and rolls
  • Witness the immersion ceremony on the last day
Aug-Sep

Onam

Kerala

Kerala's harvest festival celebrating King Mahabali's annual return. Features flower carpets (Pookalam), snake boat races, Kathakali performances, and the grand Onam Sadya feast.

Harvest Cultural Feast

Travel Tips

  • Best experienced in Kochi and Thrissur
  • Book houseboat stays well in advance
  • Don't miss the 26-dish Onam Sadya meal
  • Attend the Pulikali tiger dance in Thrissur
Aug-Sep

Ganesh Chaturthi

Maharashtra

Celebrates the birth of Lord Ganesha with grand processions, elaborate idols, and cultural performances. The festival culminates with the immersion of idols in water bodies.

Hindu Procession Community

Travel Tips

  • Mumbai and Pune have the grandest celebrations
  • Visit Lalbaugcha Raja in Mumbai
  • Try modak, Lord Ganesha's favorite sweet
  • Expect large crowds during processions
Jan

Pongal

Tamil Nadu

A four-day harvest festival where people cook the Pongal dish (rice boiled with milk) in clay pots, decorate their homes with kolam designs, and honor cattle for their role in farming.

Harvest Tamil Cultural

Travel Tips

  • Best experienced in rural Tamil Nadu
  • Witness the Jallikattu bull-taming sport
  • Try authentic Pongal dishes
  • Visit Madurai for grand celebrations

Festival Travel Highlights

Plan Your Trip

India's festival calendar is packed year-round. The best time to visit depends on which festivals you want to experience. Winter (Oct-Mar) has pleasant weather and major festivals like Diwali and Christmas, while summer brings colorful Holi celebrations.

Regional Specialties

Each region has unique festivals. North India is famous for Holi and Diwali, West for Ganesh Chaturthi, East for Durga Puja, and South for Pongal and Onam. Plan your itinerary around these regional celebrations for an authentic experience.

Festival Foods

Every festival comes with special delicacies. Don't miss Diwali sweets, Holi gujiyas, Durga Puja bhog, Onam sadya, or Pongal dishes. Many hotels and restaurants offer special festival menus - ask locals for recommendations.
